Grammar Focus

Master the modal verbs and structures used to give and ask for advice.

SHOULD / OUGHT TO → Advice

Subject + should / ought to + base verb

→ You should study harder for the Bac exam.

→ You ought to see a doctor about that.

⚠ "Ought to" is slightly more formal than "should" and is less common in questions.

IF I WERE YOU → Conditional Advice

If I were you, I would + base verb

→ If I were you, I would talk to your parents first.

→ If I were in your shoes, I'd apologise immediately.

📌 This is a Conditional Type 2 structure. Notice: "were" (not "was") is used even for I/he/she.

WHY DON'T YOU / HOW ABOUT → Suggestions

Why don't you + base verb?

→ Why don't you apply for a scholarship?

How about + -ing?

→ How about talking to your teacher?

COULD / MIGHT → Tentative Advice

Subject + could / might + base verb

→ You could try a different approach.

→ You might want to reconsider your decision.

💡 "Could" and "might" make advice sound less imposing — ideal in formal/polite contexts.

Model Dialogue

Read the dialogue carefully. Notice how advice is asked for, given, and responded to. Highlighted phrases show the target language.

📖 Situation: Youssef is stressed about his Bac results
YOUSSEF
Hey Leila, I really don't know what to do. I failed my mock exam and the Bac is in three weeks. What do you think I should do?
LEILA
I understand how you feel. First of all, don't panic. If I were you, I would start by making a study timetable. That way, you can cover every subject without feeling overwhelmed.
YOUSSEF
A timetable sounds good. But could you give me some advice on how to stay focused? I get distracted really easily.
LEILA
You should turn off your phone while you study. Also, why don't you study in short sessions of 45 minutes with 10-minute breaks? It's much more effective than studying for hours at once.
YOUSSEF
Thanks, but I've tried the Pomodoro technique before and I find it hard to stop when I'm in the zone. Is there anything else I could do?
LEILA
You might also want to join a study group. Working with others can really help. And I strongly advise you to see your teacher — I'm sure they can give you extra guidance.
YOUSSEF
That's a great idea! I appreciate your advice. I'll definitely try all of that. Thank you, Leila.
🔎 Analysis — Spot the Functions
❓
Asking for advice: "What do you think I should do?", "Could you give me some advice on…?", "Is there anything else I could do?"
💡
Giving strong advice: "You should turn off your phone", "I strongly advise you to see your teacher"
🌿
Giving soft advice: "Why don't you study in short sessions?", "You might also want to join a study group"
🚫
Qualifying advice: "Thanks, but I've tried that before…" — politely explaining it doesn't suit him
